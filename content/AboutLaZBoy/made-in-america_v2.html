American heritage

Our story

In 1928, the recliner was invented in Monroe, Michigan and La-Z-Boy was born. Our legacy of American ingenuity, drive and pride continues, thanks to the thousands of men and women across the USA who handcraft our products every day with American and imported parts. Together, we’re developing the latest innovations in comfort and convenience, while raising the bar for style and design. It’s what we do.

A legacy of quality

For over 90 years, La-Z-Boy has built the highest-quality furniture. We employ thousands of men and women across North America who cut, sew, and assemble millions of pieces of furniture each year. They share our vision of craftsmanship, innovation and American heritage, because where and how your furniture is made matters.

A spirit of American innovation

The very first La-Z-Boy was designed in a garage and crafted out of orange crates. Now, almost one hundred years later, we have changed the way America looks at comfort. The spirit of innovation remains at the heart of everything we do. From the latest comfort solutions and power options, to advances in fabrics and cushioning, our team of designers, engineers and craftsmen is committed to creating what’s next.
