Our Pledge to You, Our Customer

La-Z-Boy respects your privacy. Any information you give us about yourself is treated with care and security and should not be used in ways to which you have not consented. The information we collect is used by La-Z-Boy Incorporated, its subsidiaries, affiliates and authorized agents (referred to collectively as “La-Z-Boy” or “we/us”) for legitimate business purposes, including providing services you have requested such as furniture delivery or warranty service, recommending products to you that we think you might like, and improving your experience with our company and website. La-Z-Boy will not collect any personal information about you except as outlined in this policy. Generally, we may collect or generate the following personal information:

  1. Your full name, home address, e-mail address, phone number and other relevant contact information or user account details.
  2. The Internet protocol (IP) address used to connect your computer to the Internet; browser type, version, and time zone setting, browser plug-in types and versions, operating system, platform, and other relevant, technical details about your visit to our website.
  3. Your general geographic location, as indicated by your IP address and other connection details.
  4. Your purchase history.
  5. Uniform Resource Locator (URL) clickstream to/through/from our Web site, including date and time, cookie number, products you viewed or searched for, predictions of products you may be interested in based upon your usage of our site, and other commercial information that could help us recommend products to you while you are using our website and in the future.
  6. Images, product reviews, and other things you share with us regarding your use of our furniture, subject to our moderation policies.
  7. The general layout and design plans of certain rooms within your home that you elect to provide to us, if you are using affiliated furniture planner programs; we do not use, analyze, or review this information ourselves, it is only collected for the purposes of providing you with the room planning service for which you are using the program.
  8. Social media information you provide to us, as detailed in the next section.

Sharing Data

We only share data in two circumstances.

First, we share data in order to provide you with a service you have asked for or to obtain a service we have a legitimate interest in receiving. Examples include:

  1. Sharing your address and contact information with a delivery company providing last-mile delivery services for your furniture order.
  2. Sharing your address, contact information, and product details with a third-party furniture repair company in order to make a repair that you have requested.
  3. Contracting a professional mailing company to mail advertisements on our behalf.
  4. Using a cloud service provider as a host for one of our databases.

Second, we may contribute Personally Identifiable Information (PII) such as name, address, email, phone number, as well as, transactional history, keyed to device-identifying information such as IP and Media Access Control addresses, to a data-sharing co-op with a number of retailers with similar customer profiles across a number of industries, including high-end apparel, electronics, home improvement, etc. This co-op allows us and the other anonymous retailers to identify new prospective customers and market to them accordingly. To have your data excluded from our participation in the co-op, please consult the “Your Rights Over Your Data” section below.

Security of Your Personal Information

We have a dedicated information security team at our corporate headquarters that is responsible for ensuring the protection and integrity of your personal information. Although we cannot reveal the full details of our information security program and practices, we can reveal the following:

  1. We conceptualize our information security program and objectives using the NIST Cybersecurity Framework and other well-established, business cybersecurity standards.
  2. We use Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) encryption while your information is in transit.
  3. We contract with authorize.net for payment processing and do not retain or store your credit card or other payment details ourselves. Instead, we store a token that points to a record within their system, which we do not have direct access to.
  4. Our data is subject to reasonable and enforced retention schedules that are aligned to our legitimate interests and do not contemplate permanent retention.
  5. We use a number of other security controls and practices, including the disposal of data and information technology resources, typical to retailers and manufacturers of our size and complexity.

Cookies

La-Z-Boy uses a feature of your web browser called a "cookie" to assign a unique identification to your computer. Cookies are files that your web browser places on your computer's hard drive. We use these cookie files to track your clicks as you go through the La-Z-Boy website, to tell us whether you have visited La-Z-Boy previously, and to help us determine whether you came to La-Z-Boy from a particular internet link or banner advertisement. La-Z-Boy does not and cannot use cookies to retrieve other personal information about you (such as your name) from your computer. The "help" portion of the toolbar on most browsers will tell you how to prevent your browser from accepting new cookies, how to have the browser notify you when you receive a new cookie, or how to disable cookies altogether. However, you will not be able to use some La-Z-Boy.com features if you do not use cookies.

Rights Pursuant to the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

California law requires certain businesses to disclose information regarding the rights of California residents pursuant to the CCPA.

A California resident may request that La-Z-Boy share the following information:

  1. The categories of personal information La-Z-Boy has collected about you in the preceding 12 months;
  2. The categories of sources from which the personal information was collected;
  3. The business or commercial purpose for collecting or selling personal information;
  4. The categories of third parties with which La-Z-Boy shares personal information;
  5. The categories of personal information about you that La-Z-Boy sold to third parties in the preceding 12 months and the categories of third parties to which the personal information was sold;
  6. The categories of personal information about you that La-Z-Boy disclosed for a business purpose, as defined in the CCPA, in the preceding 12 months and the categories of third parties to which the personal information was disclosed;
  7. The specific pieces of personal information La-Z-Boy has collected about you.

A California resident may request the disclosure of the information listed above by calling 800-375-6890, or submitting a request at https://www.la-z-boy.com/content/CustomerCare/dsr. Such a request may be referred to as a right to know request. Pursuant to California law, La-Z-Boy will verify the California resident’s identity before complying with any such request.

A California resident has the right to request that La-Z-Boy delete any personal information about the California resident that it has collected about the California resident, and that La-Z-Boy direct any service provider to delete the California resident’s personal information from its records. Such a request may be referred to as a request to delete. However, pursuant to the CCPA, your information may not be deleted under certain circumstances, including where maintenance of your personal information is necessary: to complete the transaction for which the personal information was collected, fulfill the terms of a written warranty or product recall conducted in accordance with federal law, provide a good or service that you requested or that is reasonably anticipated within the context of your ongoing business relationship with La-Z-Boy, or otherwise perform a contract between La-Z-Boy and you; to detect security incidents, protect against or prosecute fraudulent or illegal activity; to enable solely internal uses that are reasonably aligned with your expectations based on your relationship with La-Z-Boy; to comply with a legal obligation; or to otherwise use your information internally in a lawful manner that is compatible with the context in which you provided the information.

For more information about these and other situations in which we may not delete your information, please see California Civil Code Section 1798.105(d). A California resident may request the deletion of information collected about the California resident by calling 800-375-6890, or submitting a request at https://www.la-z-boy.com/content/CustomerCare/dsr. Before deleting your personal information, La-Z-Boy will verify your identity.

A California resident may use an authorized agent to submit a right to know request or a request to delete. To use an authorized agent, the California resident must provide La-Z-Boy with written authorization. In addition, the California resident may be required to verify their own identity with La-Z-Boy. La-Z-Boy may deny a request from an agent that does not submit proof that they have been authorized by the California resident to act on their behalf. Such requirements, however, will not apply where a California resident has provided the authorized agent with power of attorney pursuant to Probate Code section 4000 to 4465.

A California resident has the right, at any time, to direct La-Z-Boy not to sell their personal information. This right may be referred to as the right to opt-out. You may opt out of the sale of your personal information by submitting a “Stop of Sale” request at https://www.la-z-boy.com/content/CustomerCare/dsr. If La-Z-Boy has a good faith, reasonable, and documented belief that a request opt-out is fraudulent, it may deny the request. A California resident may use an authorized agent to submit a request to opt-out on the California resident’s behalf. The California resident must provide the authorized agent with written authorization do so. La-Z-Boy may deny a request from an authorized agent that does not submit proof that they have been authorized by the California resident to act on the California resident’s behalf.

In addition, La-Z-Boy does not and will not sell the personal information of a California resident it has actual knowledge is under the age of 16 without affirmative authorization (known as “opt-in”).

La-Z-Boy will not discriminate against a California resident because the California resident exercised any of their rights under the CCPA, including, but not limited to, by:

  1. Denying goods or services to the California resident;
  2. Charging different prices or rates for goods or services, including through the use of discounts or other benefits or imposing penalties;
